Eminem

Eminem (AKA Marshall Mathers) is a popular rapper and song-writer. He was born October 17, 1972 in St. Joseph, Missouri. His parents were Marshall Bruce Mathers Jr. and Debbie Nelson, but his father left him and his mother when Eminem was a child.

As a kid, Marshall was beat up a lot. But, his love for music was first discovered when he received a soundtrack from his uncle Ronnie. Music became his escape and since he was good at it he rose to the top.

Marshall has gone through many hard times in his life with the two major struggles being that he had to go through a turbulent relationship with his now ex-wife Kim Scott Mathers and that he had to raise his daughter, Hailie Mathers, all by himself.

Marshall got his big break when he went to the 1997 Rap Olympics. He got second place, but he put on a good fight and so Dr. Dre, the record producer of Interscope Records, collaborated with him and is widely attributed to making Eminem successful.
